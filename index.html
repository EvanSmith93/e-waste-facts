E-Waste

What is it? Why is it bad? How can we help?

↓

About E-Waste

E-waste refers to any electronic product that is no longer being used. This includes computers, TVs, phones, and other electronic devices. These products are often thrown away with the regular trash, which can leach chemicals into the soil and water. When electronics are recycled, they're often sent to under-developed countries. Locals pull apart and burn the electronics to extract minerals, which releases toxic chemicals into the air.

Statistics

  • The total amound of gold in e-waste is estimated to be worth $20 Billion.
  • More than 1.4 Billion phones were sold in 2021.
  • Over 50 Million tons of e-waste is produced each year.
  • Over 80% of e-waste is not properly recycled.

How to help

The best way to help is to buy used or electronics and sell your own devices after you're finished with them. There are a number of websites that make this easy!
